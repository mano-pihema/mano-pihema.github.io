What is Emotional Intelligence?

In a basic nutshell emotional intelligence is the ability to perceive the emotions within yourself as well as the emotions of those around you. Then using these perceptions to then make informed decisions. The key skills needed to cultivate EQ are listening, empathy and reflection. In practise listening means to be mindful of the emotions of others, which are conveyed not only through spoken language but also non verbal cues. Empathy requires the listener to then try and place themselves in the speaker's shoes to gain a fuller picture of the speaker's emotional state and its context. Practising reflection after an encounter means the listener should try to be aware of their own role in the conversation and how their own emotion may have played in the encounter.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is the measure of someone's capability to solve problems involving maths, memory, visual -spatial evaluation, reasoning and logic. When a score is assigned your abilities are compared to those your own age . EQ on the other hand is about your own ability to control your own emotions in addition to understanding the emotions of those around you. Naturally it's far more personal as improvement of this nature is difficult to provide a metric for.
